## AiDux Care — Regulatory Design Source of Truth (SoT)

**Version:** 1.0  
**Scope:** Product Architecture, Prompts, UX, AI Behavior  
**Purpose:** Ensure AiDux remains a Clinical Documentation System and does not become Medical Device Software (MDR 2017/745).

---

### 1. Regulatory Intent

AiDux Care is designed as a clinical documentation assistant for physiotherapists and allied health professionals.

Its purpose is to:

- capture clinical conversations
- structure clinical documentation
- generate draft SOAP notes
- assist with insurance-grade documentation

AiDux does not diagnose, prescribe, or make clinical decisions.

All clinical reasoning, diagnosis, and treatment decisions belong exclusively to the licensed healthcare professional.

---

### 2. Regulatory Boundary

AiDux must remain outside the definition of Software as a Medical Device (SaMD) under the EU MDR.

To ensure this, the system must never perform the following actions:

**Forbidden behaviors**  
The AI must never:

- Generate a diagnosis autonomously.  
- Recommend specific treatments not explicitly stated by the clinician.  
- Prescribe exercises or therapies independently.  
- Suggest medication.  
- Suggest imaging orders as a clinical instruction.  
- Replace clinician judgment.  
- Automatically finalize medical documentation.  

If any component attempts to perform these behaviors, it must be redesigned.

---

### 3. Allowed AI Functions

AiDux AI may perform the following operations:

**Documentation support**

- transform transcripts into structured SOAP drafts  
- organize clinician statements  
- rewrite clinician decisions in structured clinical language  
- summarize patient statements  
- format treatment plans already described by the clinician  

**Clinical data description**

AiDux may describe patterns in clinical data without interpreting them as medical decisions.

Examples **allowed**:

- “Pain decreased from 7/10 to 2/10 across visits.”

Examples **NOT allowed**:

- “Treatment is effective and should continue.”

---

### 4. Human-in-the-Loop Requirement

AiDux must enforce the Human in the Loop principle.

Rules:

- Every clinical note must be reviewed by the clinician before saving.  
- The clinician must have the ability to edit every section.  
- The system must require explicit confirmation before finalization.  
- AI-generated text must always be editable.  
- No AI output may enter the medical record automatically.  

---

### 5. Separation of System Layers

AiDux architecture must maintain the following separation:

**Layer 1 – Clinical Data Capture**  
audio, transcript, inputs

↓

**Layer 2 – Clinical Documentation Engine**  
SOAP drafting, reporting

↓

**Layer 3 – Clinical Reasoning Workspace**  
patterns, red flags, analysis

Only **Layer 2** may produce content that enters the medical record.

Layer 3 must be considered **informational workspace only**.  
It must never automatically populate the clinical note.

---

### 6. SOAP Generation Rules

The SOAP generator must obey the following rules.

**Subjective**

- Summarize patient statements only.

**Objective**

- Structure examination findings provided by the clinician.

**Assessment**

- The AI may describe clinical impressions as stated by the clinician, but must not create diagnoses.

Allowed phrasing:

- “Clinical impression consistent with information documented by the physiotherapist.”

Forbidden phrasing:

- “Diagnosis: Lumbar radiculopathy”

unless explicitly provided by the clinician.

**Plan**

- The AI may structure the treatment plan documented by the clinician.  
- The AI must never invent treatment interventions.

Forbidden:

- “Recommended treatment: neural mobilization”

Allowed:

- “Plan documented by physiotherapist:
  - neural mobilization
  - strengthening exercises”

---

### 7. Language Guardrails

All prompts must enforce the following language rules.

Avoid words such as:

- “diagnosis generated by AI”  
- “recommended treatment”  
- “clinical decision by system”  

Preferred wording:

- “documentation assistant”  
- “draft note”  
- “clinician-reviewed content”  
- “structured documentation”  

---

### 8. Anti-Hallucination Rules

The system must enforce the following AI constraints.

AI must never invent:

- tests  
- findings  
- symptoms  
- treatment modalities  
- exercise parameters  

All generated content must derive from:

- transcript  
- clinician inputs  
- structured clinical context  

---

### 9. Audit Trail Requirement

AiDux must maintain traceability.

The system must record:

- AI generated draft  
- clinician edits  
- finalized note  

This ensures the clinician remains the responsible decision maker.

---

### 10. Clinical Pattern Modules

Modules such as:

- red flag detection  
- longitudinal evolution  
- narrative synthesis  

must follow this rule:

They may describe patterns but must not produce clinical recommendations.

Example **allowed**:

- “Progressive reduction in pain reported.”

Example **forbidden**:

- “Patient should continue treatment for 4 more weeks.”

---

### 11. UX Disclosure

The interface must clearly indicate that:

- AI generated the draft documentation.

Recommended UI text:

> “Draft generated by AiDux.  
> Please review before finalizing.”

---

### 12. Regulatory Safety Principle

Whenever there is ambiguity between:

- automation  
- clinician judgment  

AiDux must always **defer to clinician judgment**.

---

### 13. Engineering Rule

When developing new AI features, engineers must ask:

> “Does this feature make a medical decision?”

If the answer is **yes**, the feature must be redesigned.

---

### 14. Product Positioning

AiDux must always be positioned as:

- **AI Clinical Documentation Copilot**

Not as:

- Clinical decision system

---

### 15. Compliance Targets

The system must align with:

- GDPR / LOPDGDD (data protection)  
- EU MDR boundary for non-medical documentation software  
- Professional documentation standards for physiotherapy  

---
